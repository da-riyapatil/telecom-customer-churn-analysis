# Insights and Recommendations

## Key Insights

### 1. Overall Churn Level
The overall churn rate is **26.54%**, which means a noticeable share of customers have left the service. This makes churn an important business issue and shows the need to identify high-risk customer groups early.

### 2. Contract Type Has Strong Impact on Churn
Customers with **month-to-month contracts** have the highest churn.

- Month-to-month: **42.71%**
- One-year: **11.27%**
- Two-year: **2.83%**

This shows that customers on short commitment plans are much more likely to leave than customers on longer contracts.

### 3. Fiber Optic Customers Show Higher Churn
Churn is much higher among customers using **fiber optic internet** compared to other internet groups.

- Fiber optic: **41.89%**
- DSL: **18.96%**
- No internet: **7.40%**

This suggests that fiber optic customers are a high-risk segment and may be facing price, service, or expectation issues.

### 4. Payment Method Is Linked with Churn
Customers using **electronic check** have the highest churn rate among all payment methods.

- Electronic check: **45.29%**
- Mailed check: **19.11%**
- Bank transfer (automatic): **16.71%**
- Credit card (automatic): **15.24%**

This indicates that automatic payment users are more stable, while electronic check users are more likely to leave.

### 5. Support Services Help Reduce Churn
Customers without support-related services churn much more than customers who use them.

- No Online Security: **41.77%**
- Yes Online Security: **14.61%**

- No Tech Support: **41.64%**
- Yes Tech Support: **15.17%**

This suggests that service support features are strongly linked with customer retention.

### 6. Early Tenure Customers Are More Likely to Churn
Customers in the early stage of their relationship with the company show much higher churn.

- 0–12 months tenure: **47.44%**
- 13+ months tenure: **17.13%**

This shows that churn risk is highest in the early customer lifecycle.

### 7. Tech Tickets Are a Strong Risk Signal
Customers who raised at least one tech ticket show much higher churn compared to customers with no tech tickets.

- With tech ticket: **69.38%**
- No tech ticket: **19.69%**

This suggests that customer service issues or unresolved technical problems may be strongly linked with churn.

### 8. High-Risk Customer Combination
One especially risky segment is:

- **Month-to-month**
- **Electronic check**
- **Fiber optic**

This group has **1,307 customers** and a churn rate of **60.37%**.

This is one of the most important actionable findings in the dashboard.

---

## Recommendations

### 1. Focus Retention Efforts on Month-to-Month Customers
The company should create retention campaigns for customers on month-to-month contracts, since they show the highest churn.

Possible actions:
- offer upgrade incentives for longer contracts
- provide renewal discounts
- target these customers with retention offers before cancellation risk increases

### 2. Review Fiber Optic Customer Experience
Since fiber optic users show the highest churn, this segment should be reviewed more closely.

Possible actions:
- check whether pricing is too high
- review complaint and support trends
- improve service quality and expectation setting

### 3. Encourage Automatic Payment Methods
Customers using automatic payment methods have much lower churn than electronic check users.

Possible actions:
- encourage auto-payment through small discounts or convenience benefits
- target electronic check users with payment migration campaigns

### 4. Promote Support Services
Customers with services like **Tech Support** and **Online Security** are less likely to churn.

Possible actions:
- bundle support services into customer plans
- promote these services to high-risk customer groups
- highlight their value during onboarding and renewal stages

### 5. Strengthen Early Customer Retention
Since churn is highest in the first year, the early customer stage needs special attention.

Possible actions:
- improve onboarding communication
- provide check-in messages in the first few months
- track new customers more closely for early warning signs

### 6. Monitor Customers with Tech Tickets
Customers raising tech tickets may be showing early dissatisfaction.

Possible actions:
- follow up after issue resolution
- track repeated complaint patterns
- prioritize retention outreach for customers with recent technical issues

### 7. Use High-Risk Segment Targeting
The combination of **month-to-month + electronic check + fiber optic** shows very high churn and should be treated as a priority risk segment.

Possible actions:
- create focused retention campaigns for this group
- offer better support or plan alternatives
- track this segment regularly in dashboard reviews
